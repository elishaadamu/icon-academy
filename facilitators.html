SESAN A. PETER, PhD

Conflict management and peace studies

Profile

Dr. Sesan A. Peter is a Senior Lecturer and Researcher with Centre for Conflict Management and Peace Studies, University of Jos. He Masters and PhD in International Law and Diplomacy from University of Jos.

He is a development expert with competence in human- rights base approach. His areas of interests include domestic violence, electoral violence and internal security. He currently teaches Research Methodology for postgraduate programmes. He has engaged in training of capacity building and leadership, early warning and security intelligence gathering. He has facilitated several training in recent times, among is AfriPeace, Juritrust among others. He brings to table skills in quantitative research techniques and deep insights and experience in monitoring and evaluation of development assistance. He has engaged in research with organizations such as WaterAid, CAFOD, and Partners West Africa among others.

He has published in peer-reviewed journals among them are: “Linking Women to New Crime in Nigeria: The Emerging ‘Baby Factories and Sales” Abuja Journal of Gender Studies and Youth Advancement, Vol. 1(1 & 2), 2018, “Political and economic effects of Post-election violence on National Development”, Net journal of Social Sciences Vol. 6(2), 2019, “Containing Terrorism and Organised Criminality:

An Inclusive Approach” Journal of Peace and Development, Veritas University, Abuja Vol 2(2), 2019 and “Cultural Violence and Baby Buying in Nigeria: Structural Insecurity of Women and Children”, International Journal of Comparative Law and Legal Philosophy (IJOCLLEP) Vol 1(1) 2019, “Emerging trend of spousal violence in contemporary Nigeria”, Ibadan Journal of Peace and Development 10(1) 2020, “The impact of restorative justice on post-conflict peace building process in Plateau State”, International Journal of Comparative Law and Legal Philosophy (IJOCLLEP) 3(1) 2021. He is currently working on border security among the border communities in northern Nigeria. Dr. Peter has presented papers at workshops and conferences. He is a member of Lagos Studies Association (LSA).
